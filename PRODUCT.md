# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Three equal audiences converge on one landing page:
- **Healthcare facilities** (hospitals, clinics, urgent-care networks) looking to fill clinical vacancies fast — within 72 hours — with pre-vetted, credentialed professionals.
- **Medical professionals** (nurses, physicians, technicians) seeking their next contract or permanent position, matched by specialty, licensure, and location.
- **Recruiting partners** (staffing agencies) who want to white-label Juney's credentialing infrastructure and proprietary talent network.

Each audience should immediately recognize their entry point without scrolling. The tri-funnel is the core conversion mechanism.

## Product Purpose

Juney is a healthcare staffing platform that connects facilities with credentialed clinical professionals through a proprietary AI-driven matching engine. The landing page is the primary acquisition surface: it must convert all three audiences from first impression to signup in a single visit, while establishing Juney as the premium, trustworthy alternative to generic staffing agencies.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, tech-forward, premium. Juney speaks with clinical confidence — an expert who respects your time. Not cold, but composed. Not playful, but not stiff. The tone is a senior clinician who also happens to understand modern technology: competent, calm, and precise.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template look.** Framer/Webflow clones with identical card grids, gradient hero backgrounds, and "Trusted by 1000+ teams" badges. Juney is a real platform, not a template.
- **Playful or casual illustrations** that undermine clinical authority. No cartoon nurses, no blob people, no hand-drawn doodles.
- **Dark mode / terminal aesthetic.** Healthcare is a light-register domain. Dark surfaces suggest developer tools, not patient care.
- **Overly dense, dashboard-like layouts.** The landing page is a conversion funnel, not an admin panel. Information density should serve persuasion, not comprehension.
- **Sterile hospital-white.** Clean doesn't mean clinical-cold. The design should feel human and modern, not like a waiting room.

## Design Principles

1. **Clinical confidence, not clinical coldness.** Authority comes from precision and white space, not from sterile aesthetics. The design should feel like a private practice, not a hospital corridor.
2. **Three doors, one lobby.** The tri-funnel isn't a feature — it's the architecture. Every section should reinforce that Juney serves all three audiences with equal care and equal specificity.
3. **Show the machine, respect the human.** The AI matching engine is the differentiator, but the landing page serves real clinicians and real patients downstream. Technology is the means; human care is the end.
4. **Earned trust, not claimed trust.** Compliance badges, placement stats, and social proof are earned signals. They should feel understated and factual, not promotional.
5. **Decisive, not tentative.** Every design choice should feel committed. Bold type, committed color, generous space. Safe = invisible in healthcare staffing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum (4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text/UI components). Support responsive scaling for mobile users. Reduced-motion alternatives for all animations.
